Our story

Dumpling night should be the together part — not a pleating test.

Why STAMPLING exists

STAMPLING exists because the hardest part of dumpling night was never the cooking — it was the pleating. Hand-folding is a real skill: the first ten look fine, and number thirty-five leaks in the pot. We didn't think a family dinner should depend on that. So we made a stamp that handles the precise part: roll the dough, add the filling, dip the stamp in flour, press, twist, lift. It cuts the wrapper and seals the edge in one press, so late-batch dumplings stay as neat as the first few — and the same motion works for potstickers, gyoza, dim sum, ravioli, pierogi, empanadas, and hand pies. The night stays about the people at the table, not the technique.

Why solid maple

Every STAMPLING set holds two stamps made from solid maple wood — a small 43 mm and a large 60 mm — each engraved with the STAMPLING logo. We chose maple because it stays rigid under pressure, and even pressure is what a clean seal actually requires; thin plastic presses flex, and flex is where leaks start. Maple is also warm in the hand and made to be used again and again. Each stamp is hand-finished and individually inspected by hand before it ships. Care is simple: hand wash only, dip in flour before use, do not soak.

A small operation, on purpose

STAMPLING is sold by AJ Originals LLC, a small company in San Marino, California. We work in small batches — small enough that every stamp can be inspected by hand before it goes into the box. There is no big team behind the brand; when you write to support@trystampling.com, a real person reads the message and answers it. STAMPLING is one product in two sizes, and we would rather keep doing that carefully than grow a catalog faster than our standards.
